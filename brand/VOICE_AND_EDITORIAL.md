# Setup Sahla voice and editorial system

Version 1.0 — English launch, Egypt first

## Voice in one line

**Name the friction, check the gate, explain the fit, show the exit.**

Setup Sahla sounds like a calm expert leaning over the desk with the reader. We are useful before persuasive. We respect existing gear, the reader's budget, and the limits of our evidence.

## Five voice principles

### 1. Problem before product

Start with the symptom in the reader's language. Introduce a category or model only after the problem is clear.

- Before: “Upgrade your setup with this amazing seven-port hub.”
- After: “If one laptop port is carrying your screen, storage, and accessories, start by mapping what each device actually needs.”

### 2. Specific, not absolute

Say what a source supports and where the conclusion becomes editorial judgment. Avoid universal claims.

- Before: “The best mouse for everyone.”
- After: “Editorial fit judgment: this shape may suit someone who wants quieter everyday pointing; hand size and grip remain personal.”

### 3. Compatibility before conversion

Put the decision gate before any retailer link. Describe how the reader verifies it.

- Before: “Buy now — works with all laptops.”
- After: “Check your laptop's port capabilities and the connections your accessories require before choosing a hub.”

### 4. Dated, not live-looking

Retail prices, stock, sellers, ratings, and returns change. Call each one a snapshot and show the observation date.

- Before: “Only EGP [amount] today.”
- After: “EGP [amount] snapshot observed at [retailer] on [YYYY-MM-DD]. Check the retailer for the current price and stock.”

### 5. Useful even without a click

Each page should help the reader diagnose, check, or skip a purchase. The CTA is a next step, not the entire answer.

- Before: “You need this. Get yours before it sells out.”
- After: “Skip this if your existing setup already covers the same connection or comfort problem.”

## Tone controls

| Situation | Turn up | Turn down |
|---|---|---|
| Home and category intros | Warmth, symptom recognition | Specifications, retailer language |
| Product verdict | Directness, fit, trade-offs | Flourish, jokes, superlatives |
| Compatibility gate | Precision, sequence, source | Assumptions, reassurance without proof |
| Price/provider module | Dates, status, retailer name | Urgency, “deal” language |
| Methodology/report | Traceability, limitations | Brand slogans, persuasion |
| Social caption | One useful insight, curiosity | Claim compression, clickbait |

Light conversational phrases are welcome: “Start here,” “Check this first,” “That is the catch,” and “You may not need to buy anything.” Avoid forced slang, mock-Arabic spellings, and jokes about budgets or technical literacy.

## Page-writing sequence

Use this order whenever a recommendation page includes a commercial link:

1. **Friction:** one plain description of the symptom.
2. **Quick verdict:** who the category/model may suit, labeled as editorial fit when subjective.
3. **Compatibility gate:** evidence-backed requirements and a physical check the reader can perform.
4. **Trade-offs and skip guidance:** a reason not to buy.
5. **Affiliate disclosure:** visible before the first commercial CTA.
6. **Provider snapshot:** retailer, seller/fulfillment observation, dated price/stock if used, and link status.
7. **CTA:** named retailer and “current price” language.
8. **Evidence:** direct sources and last checked date.

## Approved building blocks

Square brackets mark fields that must come from approved canonical evidence.

### Friction opener

> If [specific symptom] keeps interrupting [task], check [reader-verifiable condition] before buying another device.

### Evidence-bound specification

> [Direct source] lists [specification] for [exact model].

Do not say Setup Sahla measured or tested it unless a documented test actually occurred.

### Compatibility warning

> Compatibility check: this fits only when [evidence-backed requirement]. Confirm [port, device, dimension, power, or operating-system detail] before buying.

For USB-C, never treat connector shape as proof of video, data speed, or charging support. State the sourced capability and tell the reader what to verify on their laptop. For stands and cooling pads, state the sourced size range and any physical fit limit. For input devices, do not turn a subjective hand-fit observation into a universal ergonomic claim.

### Skip guidance

> Skip this if [evidence-backed incompatibility or clearly labeled subjective preference].

If the reason is subjective, write: “Editorial fit judgment: skip this if you prefer [preference].” Do not hide a specification inside a preference statement.

### Price and stock caveat

> [EGP amount] snapshot observed at [retailer] on [YYYY-MM-DD]. Price, seller, stock, and returns can change; check the retailer before deciding.

If the evidence is missing or uncertain:

> Check current price and stock on [retailer].

Never reuse an old date with the word “current.” Never convert a retailer stock observation into urgency or a future prediction.

### Provider comparison

> On [YYYY-MM-DD], [retailer A observation] and [retailer B observation]. These are dated provider snapshots, not a lasting cheapest-provider claim.

“Better provider” requires evidence for the exact comparison on the named date. Otherwise describe each observation without ranking.

## Affiliate disclosure language

The disclosure must appear before the first commercial CTA, not only in the footer.

### Current launch state — direct links

Use while link status is `DIRECT_LINK — AFFILIATE ID REQUIRED`:

> **Link disclosure:** Buying links on this page currently go directly to the retailer. Setup Sahla does not yet earn from these links. Affiliate tracking may be added only when the specific program and territory are confirmed as eligible and the finished tracking link is verified.

This wording must not imply an active commission.

**Noon Egypt safeguard:** Noon Egypt affiliate eligibility is currently unconfirmed. Keep every Noon Egypt destination at `DIRECT_LINK — AFFILIATE ID REQUIRED` and use the direct-link disclosure unless owner evidence confirms an Egypt-territory program and the finished link passes verification. Affiliate availability for a different territory is not permission to monetize an Egypt link.

### Verified affiliate state

Use only when the specific program and territory have been confirmed and link status is `AFFILIATE_LINK — VERIFIED`:

> **Affiliate disclosure:** Setup Sahla may earn a commission from eligible purchases made through the marked links, at no extra cost to you. This helps fund our evidence checks.

Do not use the verified wording for a direct or disabled link. Do not promise a commission rate or program availability without current source evidence.

### Disabled state

If the link status is `LINK DISABLED — REVIEW REQUIRED`, do not render a prominent retailer CTA. Say:

> This retailer link is under review. Use the compatibility checklist while we verify the exact destination.

## Approved CTA patterns

Use sentence case and name the destination.

- `Check current price on [retailer]` (generic pattern; replace the field with the exact retailer name)
- `Check current price on Amazon Egypt`
- `Check current price on Noon Egypt`
- `View the exact listing on [retailer]`
- `Compare the dated provider snapshots`
- `Check compatibility before choosing`
- `See the evidence and limitations`
- `Find the friction in your setup`

Do not use `Buy now`, `Grab it`, `Shop the deal`, `Last chance`, or a retailer-free `Get yours`. External commercial links should use sponsored/no-follow treatment where appropriate and open safely.

## Headline and label patterns

### Headlines

- `Too few ports? Map the jobs before choosing a hub`
- `Raise the screen without replacing the laptop`
- `Cable clutter is a routing problem, not a décor problem`
- `A quieter pointer may fix more than a flashier mouse`

Question headlines must be answered on the page. Avoid putting an unsupported outcome in the headline.

### Utility labels

- `SOLVES / [symptom]`
- `CHECK FIRST / [compatibility gate]`
- `BEST FOR / [evidence-bounded fit]`
- `SKIP IF / [meaningful reason]`
- `SNAPSHOT / [YYYY-MM-DD]`
- `SOURCE / [evidence ID]`

“BEST FOR” means a bounded editorial fit within the evaluated launch set, not “best overall” in the market. If that scope cannot be stated nearby, use `A FIT FOR`.

## Arabic-aware usage

Launch copy is English, with Arabic or transliterated terms only when they help readers recognize a real query or common concept. Use standard Arabic script and mark it with the correct language/direction in digital interfaces.

- Good: `laptop stand (حامل لابتوب)` in a glossary or keyword note.
- Good: “In local searches, readers may also use [approved evidence-backed Arabic query].”
- Avoid: decorative Arabic inserted into a slogan without context.
- Avoid: assuming one term represents all Arabic-speaking markets.

Keep brand spelling as `Setup Sahla`. Do not manufacture an Arabic brand translation for the launch. Model names, USB-C, HDMI, dimensions, and evidence IDs remain exact and should be visually isolated inside right-to-left text when needed.

## Words to avoid

Avoid these unless direct evidence supports the exact scope, market, comparison, and date:

- best overall, number one, ultimate, perfect, flawless
- fastest, coolest, cheapest, unbeatable
- guaranteed, future-proof, universal, works with everything
- must-buy, essential for everyone, no-brainer
- deal, bargain, lowest price, sale (without current proof)
- only [stock], selling fast, hurry, today only
- tested by us, our benchmark, hands-on (without documented testing)
- official partner, retailer-approved, endorsed (without current authorization)

Also avoid “premium” as a substitute for describing materials or fit, “gamer” as a default audience label, and “MENA trend” when the evidence is Egypt-specific.

## Editing checklist

Before publication, confirm:

- Every factual, commercial, specification, compatibility, price, trend, or comparison statement has a direct evidence ID and source.
- Subjective fit is explicitly labeled and introduces no new facts.
- Exact model and provider destination match canonical data.
- Compatibility gate and skip guidance appear before the first CTA.
- The correct affiliate disclosure state appears before the first CTA.
- Each price/stock/seller/returns observation is dated, or omitted in favor of “Check current price.”
- No fake scarcity, copied review, invented testing, unsupported superlative, or retailer endorsement appears.
- Image alt text describes useful visual information and does not repeat decorative copy.
- English is natural; Arabic is purposeful, correctly directed, and not used as decoration.
- A reader can leave the page knowing what to check even if they never click a retailer link.
