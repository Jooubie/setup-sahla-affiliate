# Setup Sahla creative system

Version 1.0 — original launch-image directions

## The visual story: friction becomes a route

Every image starts with one visible interruption — a blocked port, a low screen, trapped heat, wandering cable, or noisy-feeling input routine — and resolves it through a clear cable-like path. The work should feel like an editorial still life built on a warm Egyptian workshop table: useful, tactile, sunlit, and carefully labeled by the surrounding page. It must not look like a retailer grid, a luxury desk fantasy, or a neon gaming advertisement.

### Shared art direction

- Palette: near-black ink, warm paper, clean cream, signal lime, and cool cyan. Small neutral graphite and brushed aluminum details are allowed.
- Composition: 70% tactile editorial scene, 30% diagram logic. Use one continuous lime or cyan cable path to guide the eye.
- Lighting: clear late-morning side light with believable soft shadows. Avoid dramatic RGB glow and high-gloss catalogue reflections.
- Camera: 35–50mm editorial perspective or clean axonometric illustration. Preserve believable scale.
- Materials: warm wood, paper labels without printed words, matte polymer, brushed metal, woven cable, neutral laptop shell.
- Product fidelity: show an unbranded *category object*, not a pixel-matched replica of the selected model. Ports and physical connections must remain plausible.
- Exclusions: no brand or retailer logos, no product packaging, no copied listing composition, no review stars, no prices, no badges, no embedded promotional text, no fake performance charts, no hands making unsafe connections.
- People: when present, show only natural working gestures and diverse adult users. Do not use a face as a substitute for explaining the setup problem.

### Output specification

| Use | Working size | Delivery | File budget | Safe area |
|---|---:|---|---:|---|
| Home/category visual | 1600×1000 | AVIF/WebP plus PNG master | 240 KB web derivative | Keep 20% clear for live overlay |
| Blog cover | 1600×900 | AVIF/WebP plus PNG master | 220 KB web derivative | Keep title outside the image |
| OG/social | 1200×630 | JPG or PNG | 300 KB | Keep essential motif inside central 1080×510 |

Retain the full-resolution original and generation record. Compression must not erase connector shapes or make cables appear discontinuous. Text, labels, prices, and CTAs are added as accessible live HTML, never baked into the generated image.

## Alt-text system

Alt text reports the useful visual information, not the marketing intention.

1. Start with the composition or action: “Overhead view…”, “Side view…”, or “Diagram showing…”.
2. Name only objects a viewer can see. A generic category object is not the exact selected model.
3. Describe the relationship that matters: cable A reaches port B; laptop is raised above keyboard; airflow ribbons stop at the underside.
4. Do not add claims, dimensions, compatibility, performance, price, mood, or quality not visible in the image.
5. If surrounding text fully communicates the same information and the visual is decorative, use empty alt text. Do not repeat the headline.
6. Keep alt text concise—usually one sentence, rarely more than two.

## Image-rights checklist

Every generated or commissioned asset must pass all checks before publication:

- [ ] Origin record includes slot ID, prompt, generator/artist, generation date, and retained source file.
- [ ] Rights status is exactly `ORIGINAL`; any other status returns to rights review.
- [ ] Do not copy retailer photographs, listing compositions, review graphics, packaging, or marketplace interface patterns.
- [ ] No Amazon, Noon, manufacturer, product, certification, or operating-system logo appears.
- [ ] The object is category-generic and does not mimic distinctive protected industrial design.
- [ ] Ports, plugs, cable direction, gravity, fan/airflow placement, and device scale are physically plausible.
- [ ] No visual promises charging, speed, temperature, adhesion, comfort, or compatibility.
- [ ] No price, stock, seller, review rating, scarcity, CTA, or promotional claim is baked into the image.
- [ ] People are depicted respectfully; no medical or ergonomic outcome is implied.
- [ ] Required crop works at desktop and mobile; essential details remain inside the safe area.
- [ ] Alt text is written or the asset is intentionally marked decorative.
- [ ] Master dimensions, derivatives, formats, and final file size are logged before handoff.

If any check is uncertain, do not publish the asset. Regenerate from the slot direction or fall back to the original cable-line mark on a brand-color field.
