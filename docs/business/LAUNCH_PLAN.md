# Setup Sahla Egypt launch plan

Campaign: **Fix the Friction: Egypt Launch**  
Launch market: Egypt first; future MENA expansion requires fresh market and program research.  
Campaign promise: **Fix the friction. Keep the gear.**

## Launch decision

Setup Sahla launches as an independent, editorial affiliate publisher for people who already own a usable laptop or PC. It diagnoses a concrete setup problem, explains the compatibility gate, and presents locally available buying options without pretending that a small accessory replaces a repair or a core device. The first release promotes five evidence-reviewed opportunities across ports, posture, thermal management, cable control, and input comfort, supported by three search-led guides.

Publishing readiness and monetization readiness are separate. The site can publish with verified direct Amazon Egypt and Noon Egypt destinations. Amazon links remain `DIRECT_LINK — AFFILIATE ID REQUIRED` until owner approval and tagged-link verification. Noon Egypt links remain direct with tracking inactive, and their eligibility record must read `Unverified — public terms do not establish Egypt eligibility` until the owner retains written confirmation.

Primary campaign objective — **TARGET:** launch the complete evidence-led site and make every public recommendation useful, traceable, disclosed, and refreshable. No traffic, conversion, commission, or revenue result is forecast.

Secondary campaign objectives:

- **TARGET:** establish a clean measurement baseline after analytics and Search Console are owner-configured.
- **TARGET:** learn which problem paths produce qualified retailer clicks without treating clicks as purchases.
- **TARGET:** complete the Amazon Egypt Associates application after the public site and owner details are ready.

### Publication and measurement gates

Publication gate: the verified build is available on the public owner-controlled domain, with required trust pages, disclosure, safe direct links, and launch-day checks complete.

Measurement gate: after publication, configure the privacy-approved analytics platform and verify Search Console before beginning a baseline or submitting the sitemap.

Analytics and Search Console are not publication blockers. They are post-publication measurement and submission gates; no performance baseline begins before they pass.

## Value proposition

**For Egypt-based laptop and PC users who want to remove daily setup friction, Setup Sahla identifies the smallest credible fix, explains who it fits and who should skip it, and compares dated local provider options with transparent evidence.**

The launch differentiates on four behaviors:

1. Start with the pain, not a product gallery.
2. Put compatibility and reasons to skip before the retailer link.
3. Compare exact Egypt listings without claiming a permanent winning provider.
4. Date unstable observations and refresh them on a defined cadence.

The launch lineup is Anker 332 USB-C hub, UGREEN 40289 foldable laptop stand, Havit F2069 cooling pad, JOYROOM JR-ZS368 cable organizer, and Logitech Signature M650 mouse. Product facts, cautions, providers, and evidence remain controlled by the canonical research package; this plan does not add product claims.

## Audiences and jobs to be done

### Primary: students and early-career laptop users

**Job:** “Help me fix ports, cable mess, heat, or uncomfortable desk use without replacing my laptop or wasting money on an incompatible accessory.”  
**Decision needs:** EGP-aware choices, exact model identity, compatibility checks, local retailer options, and a clear skip condition.  
**Likely discovery paths:** Egypt-localized search queries, bilingual problem phrasing, university and technology communities, and direct shares from friends.

### Secondary: remote workers and creators

**Job:** “Help me make a repeat-use desk setup calmer and more reliable with a small change I can validate before buying.”  
**Decision needs:** workflow relevance, provider clarity, connection requirements, and trade-offs.  
**Likely discovery paths:** search-led guides, practical short-form demonstrations, professional communities, and an owner-configured email digest.

### Tertiary: family buyer or gift buyer

**Job:** “Help me confirm that this accessory matches the recipient’s laptop and problem before I order it.”  
**Decision needs:** a short compatibility checklist, model/size labels, return-policy reminders, and no jargon-heavy sales pitch.

All audiences are in the awareness-to-consideration stage. Setup Sahla sends them to a retailer for the final transaction; it does not run a cart or checkout.

## Message system

Core message: **Keep the computer that still works; fix the part of the setup that gets in your way.**

Supporting messages:

- Diagnose first: a cooling pad is not a repair, and a hub cannot add video output when the host port does not support it.
- Check fit before price: model, port, size, handedness, cable diameter, and power requirements come before a CTA.
- Buy locally with context: retailer observations are dated snapshots, and users check the retailer for current price and availability.
- Trust the limits: Setup Sahla shows why to skip a recommendation and labels subjective fit as editorial judgment.

Channel voice is calm, direct, and Arabic-aware. Search pages stay natural English at launch while incorporating useful local terminology where it helps readers; full Arabic localization waits for native validation.

## Acquisition loops

### 1. Search diagnosis loop

Problem query → evidence-led guide → relevant compatibility section → product page → disclosed retailer click → aggregated page/query learning → guide improvement. The USB-C guide and heat guide launch first in distribution because the research found clear qualitative query patterns; this is sequence guidance, not a demand-volume claim.

### 2. Practical-share loop

One original pain-to-fix visual → concise social/community explanation → methodology or guide landing → saved/shared page → new reader question → future FAQ or article brief. Every public answer must remain useful without a click.

### 3. Refresh-trust loop

Scheduled retailer check → updated checked date, price snapshot, seller note, or disabled link → visible accuracy improvement → returning reader confidence → more qualified outbound clicks. A refresh record is proof of review, not proof that stock or price stays unchanged.

### 4. Email return loop

Useful guide → voluntary signup → periodic fix/refresh digest → return to updated guide. This loop remains inactive until the owner selects an email platform, configures consent, and approves privacy wording.

## Risk register

| Risk | Trigger | Impact | Response | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Retail price, stock, seller, or listing identity changes | Scheduled check or reader report differs from the record | Incorrect commercial presentation | Recheck exact page, qualify the observation, update the snapshot, or disable the CTA | Research/Final Review |
| Failed or redirected retailer link | Link check fails, model changes, or destination becomes unsafe | Reader harm and lost trust | Remove prominent CTA immediately and set `LINK DISABLED — REVIEW REQUIRED` until reverified | Website/Final Review |
| Noon Egypt commission assumption | Account dashboard or public copy is ambiguous | Misleading monetization claim | Keep links direct with tracking inactive and record `Unverified — public terms do not establish Egypt eligibility` until written confirmation is retained | Owner |
| Amazon application is delayed or declined | Program response or missing owner information | No tracked Amazon commission | Keep useful direct links, resolve the named deficiency, and never borrow a tag | Owner |
| Claim or product identity drifts | Retailer variant changes or evidence expires | Unsupported recommendation | Roll back to the earliest failed gate and republish only after review | Research Integration |
| Thin initial traffic | Measurement baseline remains small | Weak optimization signal | Continue problem-led owned content and technical SEO; do not infer conversion from a few visits | Business operator |
| Image-rights violation | Unapproved product photo appears in an asset | Legal/program risk | Withdraw it and use the original fallback; review the asset pipeline | Identity/Creatives |
| Analytics or consent is unconfigured | No approved platform or privacy path | Incomplete measurement | Use server/deployment logs if lawfully available; keep email/optional tracking disabled | Owner/Website |

A failed link is contained immediately when detected: remove its prominent CTA and set `LINK DISABLED — REVIEW REQUIRED` before further distribution. Complete incident documentation, owner assignment, and follow-up within one business day; that documentation SLA never delays containment.
